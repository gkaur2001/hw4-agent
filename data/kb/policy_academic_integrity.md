# Academic Integrity Policy

**Document ID:** policy_academic_integrity.md

## Overview
This course follows the JHU Academic Integrity Policy. All submitted work must be your own unless collaboration is explicitly permitted.

## What Counts as a Violation
- Submitting someone else's work as your own
- Copying code or prose from the internet without attribution
- Sharing assignment solutions with other students (unless group work is permitted)
- Using unauthorized AI tools to generate submitted work
- Plagiarism in written reports

## AI Tool Use
- Use of AI coding assistants (e.g., GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT) is **permitted for learning** and debugging, but:
  - You must disclose AI tool use in a brief note at the end of your submission.
  - You must understand everything you submit. Instructors may ask you to explain your code.
  - AI-generated content submitted as your own analysis or report writing is **not permitted**.

## Collaboration
- Homework: **Individual** unless marked as group. Discussing ideas is fine; sharing code or written answers is not.
- Group projects: Collaboration within your group only. Do not share across groups.

## Consequences
Violations are reported to the JHU Academic Integrity Board. Penalties range from a zero on the assignment to course failure and university suspension.

## Good Faith Clause
If you are unsure whether something is permitted, ask before doing it. Ignorance of the policy is not an excuse, but good-faith questions are always welcome.
