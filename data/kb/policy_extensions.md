# Extension Policy

**Document ID:** policy_extensions.md

## Overview
Students may request a deadline extension under qualifying circumstances. Extensions must be requested **before** the original deadline.

## Qualifying Circumstances
- Medical emergency (documentation may be required)
- Family emergency
- Documented technical failure on the university side (e.g., Canvas outage)
- Accessibility accommodations through JHU Student Disability Services

## How to Request an Extension
1. Email your instructor at least **24 hours before** the deadline.
2. Use the subject line: `[EXTENSION REQUEST] <Course> <Assignment Name>`.
3. Briefly describe the reason and attach documentation if available.
4. State the proposed new deadline.

## Approval
- Extensions are granted at the instructor's discretion.
- You will receive a confirmation email with the approved new deadline.
- Extensions are typically granted for **48–72 hours** beyond the original deadline.
- Extensions do not carry over; each request is considered individually.

## Retroactive Extensions
Retroactive extensions (requested after the deadline has passed) are **not granted** except in extraordinary circumstances such as hospitalization.

## Academic Accommodations
Students registered with JHU Student Disability Services (SDS) should contact the instructor at the start of the semester to discuss accommodations. Accommodation-based extensions follow SDS guidelines.
